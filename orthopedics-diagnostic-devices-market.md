<p><p>Orthopedic diagnostic devices refer to medical equipment used to diagnose and assess orthopedic conditions in patients. X-ray systems utilize electromagnetic radiation to capture images of bones and joints, aiding in detecting fractures, dislocations, and other skeletal abnormalities. CT scans employ X-rays from different angles to generate detailed cross-sectional images, enabling visualization of bones, muscles, and soft tissues. Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) employs strong magnetic fields and radio waves to create detailed images of bones, tendons, ligaments, and other soft tissues, facilitating the detection of injuries and diseases in the musculoskeletal system.</p></p>
