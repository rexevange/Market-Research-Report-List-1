<p><p>Lactic acid is a colorless liquid or powder with a slightly sour taste. It is produced naturally in the human body through anaerobic respiration and is also found in various food and beverage products. In the industrial context, lactic acid is primarily derived from natural sources such as corn, sugarcane, and cassava through fermentation processes. It is widely used in the food and beverage, pharmaceutical, cosmetic, and chemical industries.</p><p>Poly lactic acid (PLA) is a biodegradable polymer derived from lactic acid. It is produced by polymerizing the lactic acid monomers. PLA is gaining significant popularity as a sustainable alternative to traditional plastics due to its biodegradability and eco-friendly nature. It is used in various applications such as packaging, textiles, medical devices, and 3D printing.</p><p>The lactic acid and PLA market is experiencing significant growth due to increasing awareness about environmental issues and the push for sustainable materials. The food and beverage industry is a major consumer of lactic acid, driven by the demand for natural food additives and preservatives.
