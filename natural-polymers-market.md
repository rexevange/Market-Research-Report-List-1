<p><p>The natural polymers market is comprised of various types, including cellulose ethers, exudate and vegetable gums, starch and fermentation products, and other polymers. Cellulose ethers are derived from cellulose, a natural polymer found in plant cell walls, and are commonly used as thickeners and stabilizers in products like paints and cosmetics. Exudate and vegetable gums, such as gum arabic and guar gum, are plant-based polymers used in food and pharmaceutical industries for their binding and thickening properties. Starch and fermentation products, like polylactic acid (PLA), are derived from starch and microorganisms and find applications in bioplastics and textile industries. Other polymers encompass various natural substances, like chitosan and proteins, used in diverse sectors, including biomedical and agricultural fields.</p></p>
