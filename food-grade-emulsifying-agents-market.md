<p><p>Food grade emulsifying agents are substances used in food processing to create and stabilize emulsions, which are mixtures of immiscible substances such as oil and water. Ionic emulsifiers are charged molecules that work by attracting both water and oil molecules, creating a stable emulsion. Nonionic emulsifiers, on the other hand, do not have a charge and work by forming a layer around the oil droplets, preventing them from coalescing. Both types of emulsifiers are widely used in the food industry to improve the texture, stability, and shelf life of various products.</p></p>
