<p><p>Phosphine fumigant, also known as hydrogen phosphide, is a colorless, flammable gas with a pungent odor. It is commonly used as an insecticide, primarily for fumigating stored grain to control pests such as weevils, moths, and beetles. Phosphine fumigant is highly effective against a wide range of pests and does not leave any residue on the treated grains, making it a popular choice among farmers and storage facility operators.</p><p>The global phosphine fumigant market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. The increasing demand for high-quality grains and the rising need for sustainable pest control methods are driving the market growth.
