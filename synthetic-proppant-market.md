<p><p>The synthetic proppant market encompasses various types of materials used in hydraulic fracturing operations. Ceramic beads are spherical particles with high crush resistance and are commonly used due to their durability. Aluminum beads are lightweight and cost-effective, making them suitable for shallow wells. Sintered bauxite is a medium-density proppant with good conductivity. Coated sand is made by coating sand grains with resin or polymer, providing improved strength and conductivity. Other types of proppants include resin-coated ceramic beads, polymer-coated sand, and synthetic proppants made from materials like glass or ceramics.</p></p>
