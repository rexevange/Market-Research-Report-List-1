<p><p>Float glass is a type of glass that is manufactured by floating molten glass on a bed of molten metal, typically tin. The construction industry, which is one of the primary end-users of float glass, is witnessing a steady growth globally. The increasing urbanization and infrastructure development in emerging economies, such as China and India, are driving the demand for float glass in the construction sector. Additionally, the rising trend of energy-efficient buildings and the increasing demand for solar control and safety glass in both residential and commercial buildings are further fueling the growth of the float glass market.</p><p>Furthermore, the automotive industry also plays a crucial role in the demand for float glass. With the growing consumer preference for luxury and comfort features in vehicles, the demand for larger windshields and panoramic sunroofs, which extensively use float glass, is increasing.</p><p>Moreover, the market for float glass is expected to continue growing in the future. Technological advancements, such as the development of advanced coatings to improve thermal insulation and energy efficiency, are anticipated to boost the adoption of float glass in various sectors. The robust growth of the construction and automotive industries, coupled with increasing investments in infrastructure development worldwide, is also projected to drive the demand for float glass.</p><p>In conclusion, the float glass market is poised for substantial growth in the foreseeable future, with a projected compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 7.7% during the forecasted period.
