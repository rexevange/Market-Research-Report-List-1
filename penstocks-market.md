<p><p>Penstocks are devices used to control water flow in various industries. Rising spindle penstocks feature a vertically moving spindle to regulate the flow, making them ideal for applications requiring precise control. They are commonly used in water treatment plants and hydroelectric power stations. On the other hand, non-rising spindle penstocks operate horizontally without the need for vertical movement. These penstocks are often employed in applications with space constraints and are commonly found in wastewater treatment plants and stormwater systems. Both market types provide efficient water flow regulation in their respective contexts.</p></p>
