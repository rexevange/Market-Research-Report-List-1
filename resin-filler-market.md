<p><p>Resin filler is a material added to resin-based products to enhance their properties or fill voids. Glass filler refers to the use of glass particles as a filler in resin-based materials, offering increased strength, durability, and improved clarity. On the other hand, inorganic filler refers to the inclusion of non-organic substances, such as metal oxides or ceramics, in resin-based products. This enhances properties like thermal resistance, mechanical strength, and fire resistance. Both glass and inorganic fillers are commonly used in various industries to improve the performance and characteristics of resin-based materials.</p></p>
