<p><p>Bleached Softwood Kraft (BSK) refers to a type of pulp made from softwood trees, commonly used in the production of paper products. There are two main market types within the BSK category: Southern Bleached Softwood Kraft (SBSK) and Northern Bleached Softwood Kraft (NBSK). SBSK is derived from softwood trees found in the southern region, while NBSK is obtained from softwood trees in the northern region. These distinctions are important as the different types yield variations in characteristics such as strength, brightness, and fiber length, which are desired for specific paper production applications.</p></p>
