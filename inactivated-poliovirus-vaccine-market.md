<p><p>The Inactivated Poliovirus Vaccine (IPV) market comprises two primary types: Human Diploid Cell (HDC) and Monkey Kidney Cell (MKC) vaccines. HDC vaccines are produced using diploid cells from humans, while MKC vaccines are derived from the kidney cells of monkeys. These cells are manipulated to produce poliovirus antigens that are then used in the vaccine. Both types undergo similar processes to render the poliovirus inactive, making them safe for vaccination. The choice between HDC and MKC vaccines depends on factors such as safety, effectiveness, and availability in different regions.</p></p>
