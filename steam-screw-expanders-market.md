<p><p>Steam screw expanders are classified into two main market types: Type I and Type II. Type I market primarily includes applications where the expanders operate in a condensing mode, meaning the steam expands and condenses during the expansion process. On the other hand, Type II market consists of applications where the expanders operate in a non-condensing mode, typically in cases where there is insufficient cooling water available for condensation. The distinction between these two market types is based on the specific operating conditions and requirements of the expanders.</p></p>
