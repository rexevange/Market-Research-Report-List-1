<p><p>Lyocell fabric is classified into two main types: staple fiber and cross-linked fiber. Staple fiber refers to the individual fibers that are shorter in length and are spun together to form yarns. This type of Lyocell fabric is widely used in the textile industry for manufacturing various clothing items. On the other hand, cross-linked fiber involves a chemical process that strengthens the fabric, making it highly durable and resistant to wrinkles. This type of Lyocell fabric is preferred for producing products that require higher durability and longevity.</p></p>
