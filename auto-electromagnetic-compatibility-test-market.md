<p><ul><li>Bulk Current Injection (BCI)</li><li>Conducted Emissions (CE)</li><li>Conducted Transient Emissions (CTE)</li><li>Conducted Transient Immunity (CTI)</li><li>Electrostatic Discharge (ESD)</li><li>Radiated Emissions (RE)</li><li>Radiated Immunity (RI)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Auto Electromagnetic Compatibility Test (EMC) Market involves various types of tests to ensure that electronic and electrical components used in automobiles comply with electromagnetic compatibility standards. These tests include Bulk Current Injection (BCI), which evaluates the immunity of components against conducted currents, Conducted Emissions (CE) which measures the electromagnetic noise emitted by components, Conducted Transient Emissions (CTE) which tests for transient disturbances, Conducted Transient Immunity (CTI) which evaluates the ability of components to withstand transient disturbances, Electrostatic Discharge (ESD) which assesses the resistance to electrostatic discharge, and Radiated Emissions (RE) and Radiated Immunity (RI) which measure the electromagnetic radiation and immunity of components when exposed to external electromagnetic fields.</p></p>
