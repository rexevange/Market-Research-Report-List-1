<p><p>Polymer-Dispersed Liquid Crystals (PDLCs) are a type of smart material that combines liquid crystals and polymers. Scattering PDLCs feature randomly dispersed droplets of liquid crystals in a polymer matrix, which scatter light when no electric field is applied. Nano-PDLCs have smaller droplets that provide better transparency and faster switching speed. Polymer Network Liquid Crystals have a polymer network structure with liquid crystals incorporated, enabling control of the optical properties through an applied electric field. These market types offer different characteristics and applications, such as privacy window films, smart glass, and electronic displays.</p></p>
