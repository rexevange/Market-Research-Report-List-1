<p><p>The Crystallizable Shape Memory Polymer (SMP) market can be categorized into two types: Type I and Type II. Type I SMP refers to polymers that can recover their original shape and properties solely by heating, as their crystalline structure remains unchanged. On the other hand, Type II SMPs undergo a structural transformation during heating, resulting in shape recovery, and they possess enhanced mechanical properties due to the formation of crystalline domains. These two types of SMPs cater to different applications and offer varying levels of performance and functionality to meet market demands.</p></p>
