There are two main types of fluoroscopy devices: fixed fluoroscopes and mobile fluoroscopes. Fixed fluoroscopes are typically large, stationary machines found in hospitals or specialized clinics. They are designed for high-quality imaging and are primarily used for complex procedures. On the other hand, mobile fluoroscopes are smaller, portable devices that can be easily moved around. They are commonly used in emergency rooms or in situations where mobility is essential for immediate diagnosis and treatment.</p></p>

<p><strong>What are the Emerging Trends in the Global Fluoroscopy Devices market?</strong></p>
<p><p>The global fluoroscopy devices market is witnessing several emerging trends. Firstly, there is a rising demand for mini C-arms, driven by their compact size, portability, and wide application in orthopedic surgeries and pain management procedures. Secondly, there is a growing adoption of flat-panel detectors, replacing image intensifiers, due to their better image quality and lower radiation exposure. Thirdly, the market is witnessing advancements in digital fluoroscopy systems, such as the integration of artificial intelligence and 3D imaging technologies, enhancing diagnostic accuracy and workflow efficiency. Lastly, there is an increasing focus on reducing radiation exposure by implementing dose management technologies and dose tracking systems.</p></p>
