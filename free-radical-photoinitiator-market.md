<p><p>Free radical photoinitiators are chemicals used in photopolymerization processes to initiate the cross-linking reaction by generating highly reactive free radicals upon exposure to light. Cracking photoinitiators are a type that break down into free radicals during the photoinitiation process, promoting the polymerization reaction. On the other hand, hydrogen capture photoinitiators capture hydrogen atoms from a coinitiator molecule and produce free radicals, initiating the polymerizing reaction. Both types play a crucial role in initiating and promoting the photopolymerization process, enabling the formation of various polymeric materials.</p></p>
