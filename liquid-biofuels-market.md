<p><p>Liquid biofuels refer to renewable fuels that are derived from organic materials such as crops, waste, and algae. The market for liquid biofuels can be categorized into three main types: ethanol, biodiesel, and hydrogenated vegetable oil (HVO). Ethanol is produced through fermentation of sugar or starch crops and is primarily used as a gasoline additive. Biodiesel is made from vegetable oils or animal fats and is used as a diesel substitute. HVO is a renewable diesel fuel produced from vegetable oils or animal fats through a hydrogenation process, providing a greener alternative to traditional diesel.</p></p>
