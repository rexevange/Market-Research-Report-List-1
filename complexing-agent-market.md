<p><p>Complexing agents are chemicals that form complexes or compounds with metal ions, making them soluble or more stable. There are various types of complexing agents in the market. Phosphate-based complexing agents are widely used in detergents and cleaners due to their chelating properties. Alcohol amines, such as ethanolamine and diethanolamine, are commonly used in cosmetics, personal care products, and pharmaceuticals for their metal complexing capabilities. Other complexing agents include EDTA, citric acid, and polyacrylic acids, which find applications in water treatment, agriculture, and industrial processes for their ability to bind and remove metal ions.</p></p>
