<p><p>Crop input controllers are devices used in agricultural practices to manage and regulate the application of crop inputs such as fertilizers, pesticides, and herbicides. The onboard controllers are integrated into farming machinery like tractors or sprayers, providing real-time monitoring and control of input levels. Portable controllers, on the other hand, are handheld or mobile devices that allow farmers to manually adjust input parameters and monitor the application process while moving around the field. Both types of controllers offer farmers convenience, efficiency, and precise control over crop input application, enhancing crop yield and minimizing waste.</p></p>
