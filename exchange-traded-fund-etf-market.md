<p><p>Exchange-Traded Fund (ETF) Market Application refers to the ways in which ETFs are sold and traded. Direct sales involve the purchase of ETF shares directly from authorized participants, such as institutional investors or market makers. In this method, new shares are created or redeemed in response to demand. Indirect sales, on the other hand, involve trading ETF shares on a secondary market, like a stock exchange. Here, shares are bought and sold between investors, without any involvement of the ETF issuer. Both direct and indirect sales provide avenues for investors to access and trade ETFs.</p></p>
