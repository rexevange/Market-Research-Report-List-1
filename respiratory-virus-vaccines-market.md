<p><p>The respiratory virus vaccines market includes different types of vaccines, including inactivated vaccines, live-attenuated vaccines, and recombinant vaccines. Inactivated vaccines are made from killed viruses, helping the immune system recognize and fight against them. Live-attenuated vaccines contain weakened, but still live, viruses that multiply in the body, triggering a protective immune response. Recombinant vaccines use genetic techniques to insert specific virus genes into another organism, which then produces viral proteins to stimulate an immune response. These different types of vaccines provide options for preventing respiratory virus infections.</p></p>
