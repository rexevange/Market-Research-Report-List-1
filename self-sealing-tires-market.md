<p><p>Self-sealing tires are a type of tires that are designed to automatically seal punctures, thus preventing air from escaping. These tires come in two market types: diagonal tires and radial tires. Diagonal tires have a construction pattern where the internal ply cords are laid at a diagonal angle, resulting in a flexible sidewall. On the other hand, radial tires have internal ply cords that run radially from bead to bead, providing better stability and improved fuel efficiency. Both types of self-sealing tires offer enhanced safety and convenience by preventing sudden tire deflation in case of punctures.</p></p>
