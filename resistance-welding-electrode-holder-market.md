<p><p>Resistance welding electrode holders can be categorized into two types: insulated and non-insulated. Insulated holders are designed with insulation material, usually made of high-temperature resistant materials, to prevent electrical current from passing through the holder. This lowers the risk of electrical shock to the operator and reduces the potential for short circuits. On the other hand, non-insulated holders do not have any insulation material, allowing the electrical current to flow freely. The choice between insulated and non-insulated holders depends on the specific welding process and safety requirements.</p></p>
