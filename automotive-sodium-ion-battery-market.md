<p><p>The automotive sodium-ion battery market encompasses different types of batteries. One type is sodium-salt batteries, also known as Zebra batteries, which use sodium ions as charge carriers and commonly employ nickel chloride and sodium nickel chloride as electrolytes. These batteries are known for their high energy density and long cycle life. Another type is sodium-sulfur batteries, which rely on molten sodium as the anode, sulfur as the cathode, and a solid ceramic electrolyte. These batteries exhibit high energy density and are suitable for electric vehicles due to their longer range capabilities.</p></p>
