<p><p>Plug-in Hybrid Vehicles (PHEVs) come in three market types: Extended PHEVs, Parallel PHEVs, and Mixed PHEVs. Extended PHEVs operate primarily on electric power, with a small internal combustion engine (ICE) to recharge the battery as needed. Parallel PHEVs use both the electric motor and ICE simultaneously to power the vehicle. Mixed PHEVs primarily rely on the ICE, with limited electric-only driving capability. These market types describe the varying degrees of reliance on electric power and the ICE in PHEVs.</p></p>
