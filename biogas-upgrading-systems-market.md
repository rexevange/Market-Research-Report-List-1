<p><p>Biogas upgrading systems are used to purify biogas, a type of renewable energy, for various applications. There are several types of biogas upgrading systems available in the market. Membrane separation involves using permeable membranes to remove impurities from biogas. Pressure Swing Adsorption (PSA) uses different adsorbents to selectively capture impurities. Water scrubber utilizes water to remove impurities through absorption. Chemical scrubber involves using chemicals to chemically react with impurities. Other methods, such as amine scrubbing and cryogenic separation, are also used for biogas purification. These systems play a crucial role in maximizing the use of renewable energy sources.</p></p>
