<p><p>Bioplastic composites refer to a type of material that combines biodegradable polymers with natural or synthetic polymers in order to create a more sustainable and environmentally friendly alternative to traditional plastics. Natural polymer composites use polymers derived from renewable resources such as starch, cellulose, or proteins, while synthetic polymer composites use petroleum-based polymers like polylactic acid (PLA) or polyhydroxyalkanoates (PHA). Both types of bioplastic composites offer similar properties and applications as traditional plastics, but with the added benefit of being biodegradable and reducing the dependence on fossil fuels.</p></p>
