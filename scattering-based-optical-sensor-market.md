<p><p>Scattering-based optical sensors rely on the phenomenon of light scattering to measure various parameters. Raman scattering involves measuring the inelastic scattering of light, providing information about molecular vibrations and helping identify substances. Rayleigh scattering, on the other hand, measures the elastic scattering of light by small particles or molecules to determine their concentration or size. Brillouin scattering measures the inelastic scattering caused by thermal phonons, providing insight into the mechanical properties of materials. These techniques have a wide range of applications in various industries such as telecommunications, materials science, and environmental monitoring.</p></p>
