<p><p>The glycerine market can be divided into two types: crude glycerine and refined glycerine. Crude glycerine refers to the byproduct obtained during the production of biodiesel or soap. It generally contains impurities, such as methanol or soap residues. Refined glycerine, on the other hand, undergoes purification processes to remove impurities and is typically used in industries like pharmaceuticals, cosmetics, and food. While crude glycerine is cheaper due to its impurities, refined glycerine is of higher quality and more suitable for applications requiring strict standards.</p></p>
