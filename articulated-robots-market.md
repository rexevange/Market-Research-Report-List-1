<p><p>Articulated robots are categorized by the number of axes they possess, influencing their range of motion and versatility. The 4-axis or less market refers to robots limited to four or fewer rotational joints, offering relatively simpler movement capabilities. Meanwhile, the 5-axis market includes robots with an additional joint, enabling them to operate in more complex environments. The 6-axis or more market encompasses robots with multiple joints, granting them unprecedented freedom of movement and advanced dexterity. These distinctions highlight how the number of axes in articulated robots affects their operational capabilities and suitability for various industrial applications.</p></p>
